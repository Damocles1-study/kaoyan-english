When Art Meets Science: 艺术×科学合作

2022 年英语（一）真题 · Text 3 · 约 450 词 · 建议做题 18 min · 话题：文化×科研（评述文）
正确答案：31B 32A 33A 34B 35C题型：细节+例证+观点+细节+末段主旨难度：中等（34 题易错）

01原文精读

🟡 黄色高亮 = 重难点词/短语，点击右侧出卡片 ｜ 蓝色虚线 = 长难句，点击跳到精析 ｜ 每段「译」= 段落译文 ｜ 选中任意其他单词浮出「查有道」

①Enlightening, challenging, stimulating, fun. These were some of the words that Nature readers used to describe their experiences of art-science collaborations in a series of articles on partnerships between artists and researchers. Nearly 40% of the roughly 350 people who responded to an accompanying poll said they had collaborated with artists; and almost all said they would consider doing so in future.

"有启发""有挑战""令人兴奋""有趣"——这些是《自然》杂志读者在一系列关于艺术家与研究者合作的文章中，描述艺科合作体验时用到的词。在随附的问卷调查中，约 350 名受访者里近 40% 表示曾与艺术家合作过；几乎所有人都表示未来会考虑这么做。

②Such an encouraging result is not surprising. Scientists are increasingly seeking out visual artists to help them communicate their work to new audiences. "Artists help scientists reach a broader audience and make emotional connections that enhance learning," one respondent said.

这样令人鼓舞的结果并不意外。科学家们正越来越多地寻找视觉艺术家，帮助他们向新受众传播自己的研究。一位受访者说："艺术家帮助科学家触达更广的受众，并建立起增进学习效果的情感联结。"

③One example of how artists and scientists have together rocked the senses came last month when the Sydney Symphony Orchestra performed a reworked version of Antonio Vivaldi's The Four Seasons. They reimagined the 300-year-old score by injecting the latest climate prediction data for each season—provided by Monash University's Climate Change Communication Research Hub. The performance was a creative call to action ahead of November's United Nations Climate Change Conference in Glasgow, UK.

艺术家与科学家携手"震撼感官"的一个例子发生在上个月：悉尼交响乐团演奏了改编版的维瓦尔第《四季》。他们把莫纳什大学气候变化传播研究中心提供的各季节最新气候预测数据"注入"这部 300 年前的乐谱，重新构想了它。这场演出是在 11 月英国格拉斯哥联合国气候变化大会之前发出的一次创造性行动号召。

④But a genuine partnership must be a two-way street. Fewer artists than scientists responded to the Nature poll; however, several respondents noted that artists do not simply assist scientists with their communication requirements. Nor should their work be considered only as an object of study. The alliances are most valuable when scientists and artists have a shared stake in a project, are able to jointly design it and can critique each other's work. Such an approach can both prompt new research as well as result in powerful art.

但真正的合作必须是双向的。回应《自然》问卷的艺术家比科学家少；不过多位受访者指出，艺术家并不只是协助科学家满足其"传播需求"，他们的作品也不应仅仅被当作研究对象。只有当科学家和艺术家在项目中利益共担、能够共同设计、并能互相批评指正时，这种联盟才最有价值。这样的方式既能催生新研究，也能产出有力量的艺术。

⑤More than half a century ago, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology opened its Center for Advanced Visual Studies (CAVS) to explore the role of technology in culture. The founders deliberately focused their projects around light—hence the 'visual studies' in the name. Light was a something that both artists and scientists had an interest in, and therefore could form the basis of collaboration. As science and technology progressed, and divided into more sub-disciplines, the centre was simultaneously looking to a time when leading researchers could also be artists, writers and poets, and vice versa.

半个多世纪前，麻省理工学院设立了高等视觉研究中心（CAVS），探索技术在文化中的角色。创始人有意把项目聚焦在"光"上——中心名字里的"视觉研究"正源于此。光是艺术家和科学家都感兴趣的东西，因此可以构成合作的基础。随着科学技术不断进步并分化出更多子学科，该中心同时也在展望这样一个时代：顶尖研究者也可以是艺术家、作家和诗人，反之亦然。

⑥Nature's poll findings suggest that this trend is as strong as ever, but, to make a collaboration work, both sides need to invest time, and embrace surprise and challenge. The reach of art-science tie-ups needs to go beyond the necessary purpose of research communication, and participants must not fall into the trap of stereotyping each other. Artists and scientists alike are immersed in discovery and invention, and challenge and critique are core to both, too.

《自然》的调查结果表明这一趋势有增无减。但要让合作真正奏效，双方都需要投入时间，并欣然接受意外与挑战。艺科合作的边界需要超越"科研传播"这一基本目的，参与者也决不能落入互贴刻板印象的陷阱。艺术家和科学家其实都沉浸于发现与创造之中，挑战与批评对两者同样是核心。

02题目与解析（点选项即判 · 再看解析）

细节31. According to Paragraph 1, art-science collaborations have ______.

[A] caught the attention of critics
[B] received favourable responses
[C] promoted academic publishing
[D] sparked heated public disputes
答案 B　①段全是好评证据：Enlightening, challenging, stimulating, fun（四个褒义词开篇）+ 近 40% 合作过 + almost all said they would consider doing so in future → 反响良好 = favourable responses。
干扰项：A "批评家的注意"无中生有（respondents 是读者不是 critics）；C "促进学术出版"偷换（a series of articles 是《自然》的报道，不是学术出版繁荣）；D "激烈争议"反向。
技巧：首段开头的形容词群 = 全文情感基调，先给文章定调再做题。

例证32. The reworked version of The Four Seasons is mentioned to show that ______.

[A] art can offer audiences easy access to science
[B] science can help with the expression of emotions
[C] public participation in science has a promising future
[D] art is effective in facilitating scientific innovations
答案 A　例证题找例子前面的观点：②段 Artists help scientists reach a broader audience and make emotional connections that enhance learning，③段首句 One example of... 明示举例关系。把气候数据注入《四季》= 用艺术把科学传给大众 → art offers audiences easy access to science。
干扰项：B 主客颠倒——是艺术帮科学传播，不是科学帮情感表达；C "公众参与科学"偷换主题（受众听音乐会≠参与科研）；D "促进科学创新"错位——这个例子展示的是传播；"催生新研究"是④段另一码事。
技巧：例证题铁律：就例子谈例子的选项必错，答案永远是例子所服务的上位观点。

观点33. Some artists seem to worry that in the art-science partnership ______.

[A] their role may be underestimated
[B] their reputation may be impaired
[C] their creativity may be inhibited
[D] their work may be misguided
答案 A　定位④段：artists do not simply assist scientists with their communication requirements. Nor should their work be considered only as an object of study —— "不只是助手""不只是研究对象"，两个 not...only 都在抗议被看轻 → 角色被低估 = underestimated。
干扰项：B 名誉受损无中生有；C 创造力被抑制——貌似合理但原文谈的是"角色定位"不是"创造力发挥"，属于偷换概念；D 工作被误导无中生有。
技巧：Nor 开头的倒装句 = 强调句，是出题热点；两个并列否定合并归纳，就是答案。

细节34. What does the author say about CAVS?

[A] It was headed alternately by artists and scientists.
[B] It exemplified valuable art-science alliances.
[C] Its projects aimed at advancing visual studies.
[D] Its founders sought to raise the status of artists.
答案 B　⑤段功能：紧接④段"什么样的联盟最有价值"，举出半世纪前的 CAVS——光是双方共同兴趣、could form the basis of collaboration、展望研究者兼艺术家的时代 → CAVS 是有价值艺科联盟的典范 = exemplified valuable alliances。
干扰项：A "轮流领导"无中生有（原文只说展望 researchers could also be artists）；C 曲解——"visual studies"只是名字的由来（hence the name），不是项目的目标（目标是 explore the role of technology in culture）；D "提高艺术家地位"无中生有。
技巧：本题最易错。C 用原文词 visual studies 造句——原词复现≠正确，重组原词的选项反而最可疑；B 用文外词 exemplify 概括段落功能——概括性替换才是正确项的长相。

末段主旨35. In the last paragraph, the author holds that art-science collaborations ______.

[A] are likely to go beyond public expectations
[B] will intensify interdisciplinary competition
[C] should do more than communicating science
[D] are becoming more popular than before
答案 C　定位⑥段中句：The reach of art-science tie-ups needs to go beyond the necessary purpose of research communication —— 合作的边界需要超越"科研传播"这个基本目的 = should do more than communicating science（needs to → should，go beyond → do more than，教科书级替换）。
干扰项：A 偷换宾语——go beyond 的是 purpose（目的），不是 expectations（预期）；B "加剧学科竞争"无中生有（末句说 challenge and critique 是两者共同内核，不是互相竞争）；D "更受欢迎"是①段的事实且是过去时陈述，不是末段作者的主张（题干问 the author holds）。
技巧：末段主旨题盯住 needs to / must / should 等情态词所在句，那是作者最后的"喊话"。

03长难句精析（正文蓝色虚线句）

These were some of the words that Nature readers used to describe their experiences of art-science collaborations in a series of articles on partnerships between artists and researchers.
  • 开篇先甩四个形容词（Enlightening, challenging, stimulating, fun）再用 These 回指——外刊"先声夺人"式开头，读到 These 才知道前面是引语
  • that 定语从句修饰 words；注意 used to describe 是"用来描述"（use sth to do），不是"过去常常"（used to do）！
  • in a series of articles... 状语说明出处；on = 关于（articles on partnerships）
译：这些是《自然》读者在一系列关于艺术家与研究者合作的文章中，用来描述艺科合作体验的词。
The alliances are most valuable when scientists and artists have a shared stake in a project, are able to jointly design it and can critique each other's work.
  • 主干：The alliances are most valuable + when 条件性时间从句
  • when 从句内三个并列谓语：have a shared stake（利益共担）/ are able to jointly design（共同设计）/ can critique（互相批评）——"真合作"的三个标准，34 题判断 CAVS 是否典范的依据
  • a shared stake：stake 又来了！2022 T1 是 at stake（攸关），这里是 have a stake in（在…中有利害关系）——同词跨篇复现
译：只有当科学家和艺术家在项目中利益共担、能共同设计、并能互相批评指正时，这种联盟才最有价值。
As science and technology progressed, and divided into more sub-disciplines, the centre was simultaneously looking to a time when leading researchers could also be artists, writers and poets, and vice versa.
  • As 时间从句，内含 progressed 和 divided 两个并列谓语（科技一边进步一边分科）
  • 主干：the centre was looking to a time（look to 展望，≠look at）
  • when 定语从句修饰 time；vice versa "反之亦然" = 顶尖艺术家也可以是研究者
  • 句子的张力：科学越分越细（离散）vs 中心展望文理合一（融合）——正反对照
译：随着科技进步并分化出更多子学科，该中心同时也在展望一个顶尖研究者亦可为艺术家、作家、诗人（反之亦然）的时代。

05可搬进作文/翻译的素材

A genuine partnership must be a two-way street.
"真正的合作必须是双向的" —— 沟通/合作/家庭教育话题通用金句，two-way street 比 mutual 生动得多。
X and Y alike are ...
"X 和 Y 都……" —— Artists and scientists alike are immersed in discovery. 替换 both...and，alike 后置显高级。
fall into the trap of doing ...
"落入……的陷阱" —— must not fall into the trap of stereotyping each other. 批判性论证常用。
go beyond the necessary purpose of ...
"超越……的基本目的" —— Education should go beyond the necessary purpose of exam preparation. 直接套用。
embrace surprise and challenge
"欣然接受意外与挑战" —— embrace 接抽象名词是外刊标配：embrace change / uncertainty / diversity。
..., and vice versa
"反之亦然" —— 两句话说完的事三个词收尾：Teachers learn from students, and vice versa.
Such an encouraging result is not surprising.
段落承接句：先评价（encouraging）再解释原因——图表作文第二段开头可直接化用。
