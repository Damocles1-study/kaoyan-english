The Zoo Debate: 动物园该被废除吗

2022 年英语（一）真题 · Part B 新题型（观点匹配：5 人评论 ⇄ 7 个陈述选二余）· 建议做题 20 min · 话题：动物保护伦理
正确答案：41F 42C 43A 44D 45G（B、E 多余）变体：人名-观点匹配难度：中等（立场分类法可秒杀）

00解题方法论（先读这个再做题）

背景：五位读者评论 Emma Marris 的文章《The Case Against Zoos》（反对动物园）。观点匹配题的正确姿势：

  1. 先给每个人贴立场标签：反对动物园 / 支持动物园 / 中间派——只看每段第一、二句就能定；
  2. 再给七个选项贴同样的标签：F 是"该关"（反）；A/D/E/G 是"有价值"（支持）；B/C 带让步（中间）；
  3. 立场对上后，再用关键词复现锁定：钱（bottom line ↔ moneymaking）、教育（major in ↔ path of sciences）、替人跑腿（travel to wild ↔ save trips）、研究被歪曲（misrepresent ↔ distort）；
  4. 多余的两项通常是：无人提及的具体主张（E 的"最佳替代方案"）和偷换的极端说法（B 的"少花钱"）。

01七个选项（A–G）

02五位评论者 · 点字母作答

🟡 高亮词可点出词卡 ｜ 红色虚线 = 与正确选项对应的线索句 ｜ 每人下方点 A–G 作答，答后出线索链解析

(41) Teri Byrd 立场：反对动物园

I was a zoo and wildlife park employee for years. Both the wildlife park and zoo claimed to be operating for the benefit of the animals and for conservation purposes. This claim was false. Neither one of them actually participated in any contributions to animal research or conservation. They are profitable institutions whose bottom line is much more important than the condition of the animals.

Animals despise being captives in zoos. No matter how you "enhance" enclosures, they do not allow for freedom, a natural diet or adequate exercise. Animals end up stressed and unhealthy or dead. It's past time for transparency with these institutions, and it's past time to eliminate zoos from our culture.

我当过多年动物园和野生动物园员工。两者都声称自己的运营是为了动物福利和保护目的——这是假话。它们没有真正为动物研究或保护做过任何贡献；它们是逐利机构，账本盈亏远比动物的状况重要。动物憎恶在动物园里被囚禁。不管你怎么"升级"围栏，都给不了自由、天然饮食和足量运动。动物最终不是紧张憔悴就是死亡。早就该让这些机构透明化了，也早就该把动物园从我们的文化中清除出去了。
41 → F　线索链：profitable institutions whose bottom line is much more important than the condition of the animals ↔ F 的 prioritize moneymaking over animals' well-being（bottom line→moneymaking，condition→well-being）；past time to eliminate zoos ↔ should have been closed down（eliminate→close down，past time→should have been 的"早该"语气）。全场唯一喊"关掉"的人对应唯一喊"关掉"的选项。
(42) Karen R. Sime 立场：中间派（让步+肯定教育价值）

As a zoology professor, I agree with Emma Marris that zoo displays can be sad and cruel. But she underestimates the educational value of zoos.

The zoology program at my university attracts students for whom zoo visits were the crucial formative experience that led them to major in biological sciences. These are mostly students who had no opportunity as children to travel to wilderness areas, wildlife refuges or national parks. Although good TV shows can help stir children's interest in conservation, they cannot replace the excitement of a zoo visit as an intense, immersive and interactive experience. Surely there must be some middle ground that balances zoos' treatment of animals with their educational potential.

作为动物学教授，我同意 Emma Marris 所说：动物园的展出可能悲哀而残忍。但她低估了动物园的教育价值。我所在大学的动物学专业吸引的学生，很多人正是因为儿时参观动物园的关键塑造性经历才选择主修生物科学。这些学生大多小时候没有机会去荒野、野生动物保护区或国家公园。好的电视节目虽有助于激发孩子的保护兴趣，却无法取代动物园之行那种强烈、沉浸、互动的体验。当然，一定存在某种中间地带，能在动物园对动物的对待与其教育潜力之间取得平衡。
42 → C　线索链：C 前半 While animals in captivity deserve sympathy ↔ 她的让步 I agree ... can be sad and cruel；C 后半 starting young people down the path of related sciences ↔ formative experience that led them to major in biological sciences（path of related sciences→major in biological sciences）。While 让步结构与"agree...But..."的行文完美同构——立场标签法一步到位。
(43) Greg Newberry 立场：强烈支持动物园（斥责 Marris）

Emma Marris's article is an insult and a disservice to the thousands of passionate, dedicated people who work tirelessly to improve the lives of animals and protect our planet. She uses outdated research and decades-old examples to undermine the noble mission of organizations committed to connecting children to a world beyond their own.

Zoos are at the forefront of conservation and constantly evolving to improve how they care for animals and protect each species in its natural habitat. Are there tragedies? Of course. But they are the exception, not the norm that Ms. Marris implies. A distressed animal in a zoo will get as good or better treatment than most of us at our local hospital.

Emma Marris 的文章是对成千上万满怀热情、兢兢业业改善动物生活、保护地球的人的侮辱和伤害。她用过时的研究和几十年前的旧例，来贬损那些致力于把孩子与更广阔世界相连的组织的崇高使命。动物园站在保护事业的最前沿，并在不断进化以改进对动物的照料、保护各物种的自然栖息地。有悲剧吗？当然有。但那是例外，不是 Marris 暗示的常态。动物园里一只状态不佳的动物得到的治疗，不会比我们大多数人在本地医院得到的差，甚至更好。
43 → A　线索链：A 的 spare no effort to take care of animals ↔ constantly evolving to improve how they care for animals + as good or better treatment than ... hospital；A 的 should not be subjected to unfair criticism ↔ 整段对 Marris 的反击（insult / outdated research / undermine）——"文章是侮辱" = "批评不公正"。五人中骂得最狠的配语气最强的 A。
(44) Dean Gallea 立场：支持（环保者的实用主义辩护）

As a fellow environmentalist, animal-protection advocate and longtime vegetarian, I could properly be in the same camp as Emma Marris on the issue of zoos. But I believe that well-run zoos, and the heroic animals that suffer their captivity, do serve a higher purpose. Were it not for opportunities to observe these beautiful, wild creatures close to home, many more people would be driven by their fascination to travel to wild areas to seek out, disturb and even hunt them down.

Zoos are, in that sense, similar to natural history and archaeology museums, serving to satisfy our need for contact with these living creatures while leaving the vast majority undisturbed in their natural environments.

作为同样的环保主义者、动物保护倡导者和多年素食者，在动物园问题上我本可以和 Emma Marris 站在同一阵营。但我相信，管理良好的动物园——以及忍受圈养之苦的英雄动物们——确实服务于一个更高的目的：若不是在家门口就有机会观察这些美丽的野生动物，会有更多人被好奇心驱使跑到野外去寻找、打扰甚至猎杀它们。在这个意义上，动物园类似自然历史博物馆和考古博物馆：既满足我们接触这些活物的需求，又让绝大多数动物在自然环境中不受打扰。
44 → D　线索链：D 的 save people trips to wilderness areas ↔ Were it not for opportunities to observe ... close to home, many more people would ... travel to wild areas（虚拟语气反过来说 = 动物园省去了这些远行）；D 的 contribute to wildlife conservation ↔ leaving the vast majority undisturbed in their natural environments。⚠️ 别选 E——他说的是"就近观察替代远行"，不是"无法放归的动物的最佳去处"。
(45) John Fraser 立场：支持（被引用研究的作者亲自下场）

Emma Marris selectively describes and misrepresents the findings of our research. Our studies focused on the impact of zoo experiences on how people think about themselves and nature, and the data points extracted from our studies do not, in any way, discount what is learned in a zoo visit.

Zoos are tools for thinking. Our research provides strong support for the value of zoos in connecting people with animals and with nature. Zoos provide a critical voice for conservation and environmental protection. They afford an opportunity for people from all backgrounds to encounter a range of animals, from drone bees to springbok or salmon, to better understand the natural world we live in.

Emma Marris 有选择地描述并歪曲了我们研究的发现。我们的研究关注动物园经历如何影响人们看待自我与自然的方式，而从我们研究中提取的数据点绝不贬低动物园参观中学到的东西。动物园是思考的工具。我们的研究有力支持了动物园在连接人与动物、人与自然方面的价值。动物园为保护事业和环保发出关键声音，让各种背景的人都有机会接触从雄蜂到跳羚、鲑鱼的各类动物，从而更好地理解我们生活的自然世界。
45 → G　线索链：G 的 Marris distorts our findings ↔ selectively describes and misrepresents the findings of our research（misrepresent→distort，教科书级替换）；G 的 zoos serve as an indispensable link between man and nature ↔ the value of zoos in connecting people with animals and with nature（connect→link）。"our research"是身份指纹——只有他是研究作者本人。

03两个多余项为什么不能选

B. To pressure zoos to spend less on their animals would lead to inhumane outcomes...
"迫使动物园少花钱会导致不人道后果" —— 无中生有+偷换：没有任何人讨论"给动物园施压让它少花钱"。Byrd 批评的是动物园赚钱不干实事，方向完全相反。典型的"用原文词造没人说过的话"。
E. For wild animals that cannot be returned to their natural habitats, zoos offer the best alternative.
"对无法放归野外的动物，动物园是最佳替代" —— 貌似合理但无人提及：44 Gallea 说的是"就近看动物省得人跑去野外打扰"，主语是人的行程；E 的主语是无法放归的动物的去处。最强干扰项，专钓"感觉像"不核对主语的人。

05可搬进作文/翻译的素材

Were it not for ..., many more people would ...
"若不是……，更多人将会……" —— 虚拟语气倒装（= If it were not for），写作提档神器：Were it not for public libraries, many children would have no access to books.
They are the exception, not the norm.
"那是例外，不是常态" —— 反驳以偏概全的万能句；norm 复现（T1 的 unquestioned norm）。
Surely there must be some middle ground that balances A with B.
"一定存在平衡 A 与 B 的中间地带" —— 议论文结尾中立立场句，balance A with B 搭配照抄。
It's past time to do ...
"早就该……了" —— 比 It's time to 更急迫；It's past time for transparency. 呼吁类话题可用。
an insult and a disservice to ...
"对……的侮辱和伤害" —— do sb a disservice 帮倒忙/有损于，反驳时的强语气组合。
X is at the forefront of ...
"X 站在……最前沿" —— China is at the forefront of renewable energy. 科技/发展话题高频。
