The Dismissal Paradox: 解雇保护的悖论

2022 年英语（一）真题 · Text 4 · 约 470 词 · 建议做题 20 min · 话题：法律×经济（新西兰《雇佣关系法》）
正确答案：36D 37A 38D 39B 40C题型：细节+细节+推断+因果+推断难度：偏难（2022 最难篇，逻辑链长）

01原文精读

🟡 黄色高亮 = 重难点词/短语，点击右侧出卡片 ｜ 蓝色虚线 = 长难句，点击跳到精析 ｜ 每段「译」= 段落译文 ｜ 选中任意其他单词浮出「查有道」
💡 全文逻辑主线：保护普通工人的法律（②）→ 误伤企业（③④⑤）→ 反噬经理和工人自己（⑥）→ 拖累全社会（⑦）→ 澳新两国的补救尝试（⑧）

①The personal grievance provisions of New Zealand's Employment Relations Act 2000 (ERA) prevent an employer from firing an employee without good cause. Instead, dismissals must be justified. Employers must both show cause and act in a procedurally fair way.

新西兰《2000 年雇佣关系法》（ERA）中的个人申诉条款禁止雇主无正当理由解雇员工。相反，解雇必须有正当依据：雇主既要说明理由，又要在程序上做到公平。

②Personal grievance procedures were designed to guard the jobs of ordinary workers from "unjustified dismissals". The premise was that the common law of contract lacked sufficient safeguards for workers against arbitrary conduct by management. Long gone are the days when a boss could simply give an employee contractual notice.

个人申诉程序的设计初衷，是保护普通工人的工作免遭"不正当解雇"。其前提是：普通合同法未能为工人提供足够保障，难以抵御管理层的专断行为。老板一纸合同通知就能打发员工走人的日子早已一去不返。

③But these provisions create difficulties for businesses when applied to highly paid managers and executives. As countless boards and business owners will attest, constraining firms from firing poorly performing, high-earning managers is a handbrake on boosting productivity and overall performance. The difference between C-grade and A-grade managers may very well be the difference between business success or failure. Between preserving the jobs of ordinary workers or losing them. Yet mediocrity is no longer enough to justify a dismissal.

但当这些条款用到高薪经理和高管身上时，就给企业制造了难题。正如无数董事会和企业主可以作证的：禁止公司解雇表现糟糕的高薪经理，就是拉住生产率和整体绩效提升的一记手刹。C 级经理和 A 级经理之差，很可能就是企业成败之差——也是普通工人保住饭碗还是丢掉饭碗之差。然而如今，"平庸"已不足以构成解雇的正当理由。

④Consequently—and paradoxically—laws introduced to protect the jobs of ordinary workers may be placing those jobs at risk.

于是——悖论出现了——为保护普通工人工作而出台的法律，可能恰恰在把这些工作置于险境。

⑤If not placing jobs at risk, to the extent employment protection laws constrain business owners from dismissing underperforming managers, those laws act as a constraint on firm productivity and therefore on workers' wages. Indeed, in "An International Perspective on New Zealand's Productivity Paradox" (2014), the Productivity Commission singled out the low quality of managerial capabilities as a cause of the country's poor productivity growth record.

即便不至于让岗位消失，只要雇佣保护法在多大程度上限制企业主解雇不称职的经理，它就在多大程度上约束着企业生产率，进而压制工人工资。事实上，生产率委员会在《新西兰生产率悖论的国际视角》（2014）中，就把管理能力低下单独点名为该国生产率增长记录糟糕的一个原因。

⑥Nor are highly paid managers themselves immune from the harm caused by the ERA's unjustified dismissal procedures. Because employment protection laws make it costlier to fire an employee, employers are more cautious about hiring new staff. This makes it harder for the marginal manager to gain employment. And firms pay staff less because firms carry the burden of the employment arrangement going wrong.

高薪经理们自己也无法幸免于 ERA 不正当解雇程序带来的伤害。由于雇佣保护法让解雇变得更昂贵，雇主招新人时会更加谨慎，这让处于录用边缘的经理更难找到工作。而且，因为"雇错人"的风险要企业自己扛，企业会普遍压低员工薪酬。

⑦Society also suffers from excessive employment protections. Stringent job dismissal regulations adversely affect productivity growth and hamper both prosperity and overall well-being.

全社会也在为过度的雇佣保护买单：严苛的解雇规定不利于生产率增长，妨碍繁荣与整体福祉。

⑧Across the Tasman Sea, Australia deals with the unjustified dismissal paradox by excluding employees earning above a specified "high-income threshold" from the protection of its unfair dismissal laws. In New Zealand, a 2016 private members' Bill tried to permit firms and high-income employees to contract out of the unjustified dismissal regime. However, the mechanisms proposed were unwieldy and the Bill was voted down following the change in government later that year.

在塔斯曼海对岸，澳大利亚的解法是：把收入超过特定"高收入门槛"的雇员排除在不公平解雇法的保护之外。在新西兰，2016 年一项议员私人法案曾试图允许企业与高收入雇员通过合同约定退出不正当解雇制度；然而所提机制笨重难行，加上当年晚些时候政府更迭，法案被否决了。

02题目与解析（点选项即判 · 再看解析）

细节36. The personal grievance provisions of the ERA are intended to ______.

[A] punish dubious corporate practices
[B] improve traditional hiring procedures
[C] exempt employers from certain duties
[D] protect the rights of ordinary workers
答案 D　定位①②段：prevent an employer from firing an employee without good cause + designed to guard the jobs of ordinary workers from "unjustified dismissals"。intended to = designed to（题干与原文的信号词对接），guard → protect。
干扰项：A 偷换——条款是"预防解雇"不是"惩罚企业"；B 张冠李戴——管的是解雇（dismissal）不是招聘（hiring）；C 反向——是给雇主增加义务（must show cause），不是免除。
技巧：问立法目的 → 找 designed to / intended to / aim 后面的内容，通常在制度介绍段（前两段）。

细节37. It can be learned from Paragraph 3 that the provisions may ______.

[A] hinder business development
[B] undermine managers' authority
[C] affect the public image of the firms
[D] worsen labor-management relations
答案 A　③段首句 create difficulties for businesses + 比喻句 a handbrake on boosting productivity and overall performance（手刹=阻碍）+ the difference between business success or failure → 妨碍企业发展 = hinder business development。handbrake → hinder，比喻转直义。
干扰项：B "削弱经理权威"无中生有（经理是被保护的对象）；C 公众形象未提；D 劳资关系未提。
技巧：比喻词（handbrake）几乎必出题——答案是比喻的"翻译"（hinder/obstacle），不是比喻本身。与 T1 的 time bomb → inherently flawed 同理。

推断38. Which of the following measures would the Productivity Commission support?

[A] Imposing reasonable wage restraints.
[B] Enforcing employment protection laws.
[C] Limiting the powers of business owners.
[D] Dismissing poorly performing managers.
答案 D　推断链：⑤段 the Productivity Commission singled out the low quality of managerial capabilities as a cause of the country's poor productivity growth —— 既然该委员会认定"管理能力低下"拖累生产率，那么它自然支持"能把不行的经理换掉" = Dismissing poorly performing managers。
干扰项：A "工资限制"无中生有（工资被压低是保护法的恶果，不是谁主张的措施）；B 反向——保护法正是全文批判的对象；C 反向——现状就是企业主权力被限（constrain business owners），委员会立场恰恰相反。
技巧："某机构会支持什么"型推断题：先找该机构的原文立场（一句话），再顺着立场推一步，不能推两步。

因果细节39. What might be an effect of ERA's unjustified dismissal procedures?

[A] Highly paid managers lose their jobs.
[B] Employees suffer from salary cuts.
[C] Society sees a rise in overall well-being.
[D] Employers need to hire new staff.
答案 B　⑥段因果链：解雇成本高 → 招聘更谨慎 → 边缘经理难就业 → firms pay staff less because firms carry the burden of the employment arrangement going wrong —— 企业把"雇错人"的风险成本转嫁成低工资 = Employees suffer from salary cuts（⑤段 a constraint ... on workers' wages 双重印证）。
干扰项：A 反向——高薪经理是难被解雇也难被雇，不是"失业"；C 反向——⑦段 hamper well-being；D 反向——是 more cautious about hiring（更不敢招人）。
技巧：问 effect 的题，沿着 Because.../This makes.../And... 的因果链走到底，答案常在链条末端。

推断40. It can be inferred that the "high-income threshold" in Australia ______.

[A] has secured managers' earnings
[B] has produced undesired results
[C] is beneficial to business owners
[D] is difficult to put into practice
答案 C　⑧段：澳洲把高收入雇员排除在不公平解雇法保护之外 → 企业主可以自由处置高薪经理 → 全文诟病的"手刹"被松开 → 对企业主有利 = beneficial to business owners（作者通篇站在生产率/企业效率一侧，把澳洲做法作为正面解法介绍）。
干扰项：A 反向——门槛之上的经理失去保护，收入更没保障；B 无中生有——文中没说澳洲方案产生不良后果；D 张冠李戴——"难以推行"的是新西兰 2016 法案（unwieldy、被否决），不是澳洲的门槛制度。
技巧：末段出现两个国家/两个方案时，必有一题考"张冠李戴"——把 NZ 的失败嫁接到澳洲头上，D 就是这么造出来的。

03长难句精析（正文蓝色虚线句）

Long gone are the days when a boss could simply give an employee contractual notice.
  • 完全倒装：正常语序 The days when... are long gone；把表语 Long gone 提前，强调"一去不返"
  • when 定语从句修饰 the days
  • 写作直接可搬的金句模板：Long gone are the days when + 旧现象
译：老板一纸合同通知就能打发员工的日子，早已一去不复返。
As countless boards and business owners will attest, constraining firms from firing poorly performing, high-earning managers is a handbrake on boosting productivity and overall performance.
  • As 引导非限定性定语从句（"正如……可以作证的"），attest 熟词：at+test 出庭作证→证实
  • 主语是动名词短语：constraining firms from firing...（禁止企业解雇……这件事）；firing 的宾语带两个前置定语 poorly performing（表现差的）+ high-earning（高薪的）
  • a handbrake on doing：手刹比喻，37 题考点——比喻转直义 = obstacle/hindrance
译：正如无数董事会和企业主可以作证的：禁止企业解雇表现糟糕的高薪经理，就是拉住生产率与整体绩效提升的一记手刹。
If not placing jobs at risk, to the extent (that) employment protection laws constrain business owners from dismissing underperforming managers, those laws act as a constraint on firm productivity and therefore on workers' wages.
  • If not placing jobs at risk：省略主语的条件短语（= even if they do not place jobs at risk），承接④段"置岗位于险境"的说法退一步
  • to the extent (that)...："在……的程度上/只要……"，that 被省略——本句最大读障
  • 主干：those laws act as a constraint on A and (therefore) on B——on 介词并列，therefore 插在中间表递进因果：约束生产率进而压工资
  • 39 题 B（salary cuts）的第一处证据就在句尾
译：即便不至于让岗位消失，只要雇佣保护法限制企业主解雇不称职经理，它就构成对企业生产率的约束，进而压制工人的工资。

05可搬进作文/翻译的素材

Long gone are the days when ...
"……的日子一去不返" —— 倒装开头，今昔对比类作文首段王牌：Long gone are the days when a diploma guaranteed a job.
Consequently—and paradoxically—...
"于是——而且悖论的是——" —— 双破折号插入评价，指出好心办坏事：Policies meant to help may—paradoxically—hurt.
X is a handbrake on ...
"X 是……的手刹" —— 比喻"阻碍"，替换 hinder/impede：Excessive homework is a handbrake on creativity.
Nor are X themselves immune from ...
"X 自己也不能幸免于……" —— Nor 倒装 + immune from，连锁影响论证利器：Nor are adults themselves immune from screen addiction.
single out X as ...
"把 X 单独点名为……" —— The report singled out inefficiency as the main cause. 替换 identify。
hamper both prosperity and overall well-being
"妨碍繁荣与整体福祉" —— hamper/adversely affect/stringent 一组负面政策评价词，社会类话题通用。
Society also suffers from ...
"全社会也为……付出代价" —— 论证升维句：个人→企业→社会，层层推进的第三层开头。
