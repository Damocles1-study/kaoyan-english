The Four-Day Week: Liberation or Compression?

01原文精读

①In the summer of 2022, more than sixty British companies embarked on what was then the world's largest trial of a four-day working week. Employees kept their full salaries while cutting their hours by a fifth, on the condition that they maintained 100 percent of their output. When the results were published, the enthusiasm was hard to contain: revenues held steady, resignations fell sharply, and the overwhelming majority of firms vowed never to look back.

2022 年夏天，六十多家英国公司启动了当时全球最大规模的四天工作制试验。员工薪资不变、工时削减五分之一，条件是必须维持 100% 的产出。结果公布时，各方的热情几乎难以抑制：营收保持稳定，离职率大幅下降，绝大多数公司发誓再也不走回头路。

②Such findings feed a seductive narrative — that the five-day week is merely a relic of the industrial age, ripe for abolition. Advocates point out that the two-day weekend was itself an invention, resisted fiercely by employers a century ago before becoming an unquestioned norm. If society could compress six working days into five without economic collapse, they argue, compressing five into four is simply the next step in a long historical march toward less toil.

这些发现喂养了一种极具诱惑力的论调——五天工作制不过是工业时代的遗物，早就该废除了。倡导者指出，双休日本身也是一项"发明"：一个世纪前曾遭雇主激烈抵制，后来才成为无人质疑的常规。他们主张，既然社会能把六个工作日压缩成五天而没有导致经济崩溃，那么五天压成四天不过是"减少辛劳"这场漫长历史进程中的下一步。

③Yet skeptics urge caution before declaring victory. The companies that volunteered for the trial were, by definition, those most confident of success: small, white-collar and digitally nimble. A hospital ward or an assembly line cannot condense its schedule by merely trimming meetings and silencing notifications. For sectors where output is tied to hours of physical presence, a shorter week means either hiring more staff or serving fewer people — both of which carry costs that someone must eventually absorb.

然而，怀疑者提醒：先别急着宣布胜利。自愿参加试验的公司，本质上就是那些最有把握成功的公司——规模小、白领型、数字化转身灵活。医院病房或流水线不可能靠"少开会、关通知"来压缩排班。对于产出与"人在场时长"直接挂钩的行业，一周变短意味着要么多雇人、要么少服务——两者都有代价，而这代价最终总得有人来承担。

④There is also a subtler worry. Some researchers observed that employees in the trial, though happier on the whole, reported working at a noticeably intensified pace, cramming five days of tasks into four. What is marketed as liberation, critics contend, may in practice amount to compression — the same burden squeezed into a narrower container, with the pressure merely redistributed rather than relieved.

还有一个更隐蔽的担忧。有研究者观察到，试验中的员工虽然总体上更快乐，却报告说工作节奏明显加快——把五天的任务硬塞进四天。批评者主张：被包装成"解放"的东西，实际上可能只是"压缩"——同样的负担被挤进一个更窄的容器，压力只是被重新分配了，而非真正减轻。

⑤The sensible conclusion is neither utopian nor dismissive. The four-day week is not a universal remedy, but the trials have punctured the assumption that hours worked and value created move in lockstep. Whether other industries can follow will depend less on ideology than on a patient, sector-by-sector reckoning with what work actually consists of — a question the five-day week allowed us, for a century, to avoid asking.

明智的结论既不乌托邦式空想，也不一味否定。四天工作制不是万能药，但这些试验已经戳破了一个假设：工作时长与创造的价值同步变动。其他行业能否跟进，与其说取决于理念之争，不如说取决于一场耐心的、逐行业的审视——工作究竟由什么构成？这个问题，五天工作制让我们整整回避了一个世纪。

02题目与解析（点选项即判 · 再看解析）

21. According to Paragraph 1, the British trial of a four-day week ______.

[A] required employees to accept reduced salaries
[B] demanded that productivity remain undiminished
[C] was triggered by a sharp rise in resignations
[D] failed to keep company revenues stable
细节题 · 答案 B　定位①段：on the condition that they maintained 100 percent of their output —— "维持100%产出"= productivity remain undiminished（同义替换：output→productivity, maintained 100%→undiminished）。
干扰项：A 反了（kept their full salaries）；C 因果倒置（离职率下降是结果不是起因）；D 反了（revenues held steady）。

22. Advocates mention the two-day weekend in order to show that ______.

[A] shortening work time has a historical precedent
[B] employers invariably stand in the way of progress
[C] the industrial age has definitively come to an end
[D] inventions are usually resisted before acceptance
例证题 · 答案 A　例证题问的永远是"例子服务于什么观点"。观点在例子前：the five-day week is merely a relic... ripe for abolition，双休日的例子是为了论证"工时缩短在历史上发生过、也成功过"→ 有先例（precedent）。
干扰项：B "invariably"（一概如此）过于绝对；C 无中生有；D 把例子细节当观点——例证题最经典的坑：就例子谈例子。

23. Which of the following is a limitation of the trial according to Paragraph 3?

[A] Its participants were self-selected and unrepresentative.
[B] Hospitals and factories refused to release their data.
[C] It cut meetings without improving communication.
[D] It forced firms to hire more staff at high costs.
细节/推断题 · 答案 A　定位③段：The companies that volunteered... were, by definition, those most confident of success: small, white-collar and digitally nimble —— 自愿报名（volunteered→self-selected）且类型单一（→unrepresentative，样本偏差）。
干扰项：B 原文说医院"无法压缩排班"，不是"拒绝交数据"（偷换）；C 拼接原文词语造句；D 是对假设情形的推演（means either...or...），不是试验本身的做法。

24. The word "compression" (Para. 4) most probably means ______.

[A] a cut in pay proportional to hours
[B] a decline in workers' overall happiness
[C] the same workload packed into less time
[D] the transfer of tasks to other employees
猜词题 · 答案 C　猜词看破折号后的同位解释：the same burden squeezed into a narrower container —— 同样的负担塞进更窄的容器 = 同样工作量装进更少时间。前文 cramming five days of tasks into four 也是同一意思的具体化。
技巧：猜词题答案≈上下文的同义改写，破折号/冒号/即 that is 后面就是"官方解释"。

25. Which of the following best describes the author's attitude toward the four-day week?

[A] Enthusiastic endorsement
[B] Outright rejection
[C] Total indifference
[D] Reserved approval
态度题 · 答案 D　定位⑤段首句：neither utopian nor dismissive（既不空想也不否定）+ not a universal remedy, but the trials have punctured the assumption... —— 承认价值但有保留 = reserved approval（保留的赞同）。
态度题词库：reserved/qualified approval 有保留的赞同（正确答案高频）；objective 客观；skeptical 怀疑 ｜ 基本不选：indifferent 漠不关心、biased 偏见、subjective 主观臆断。

03长难句精析（正文蓝色虚线句）

Employees kept their full salaries while cutting their hours by a fifth, on the condition that they maintained 100 percent of their output.
  • 主干：Employees kept their full salaries（员工保有全薪）
  • while + doing：伴随状语，"同时削减工时五分之一"（by a fifth = by 20%，by 表差额）
  • on the condition that...：条件状语从句，"条件是……"——写作可直接搬用的句型
译：员工薪资不变、工时削减五分之一，条件是维持 100% 的产出。
Advocates point out that the two-day weekend was itself an invention, resisted fiercely by employers a century ago before becoming an unquestioned norm.
  • 主干：Advocates point out that + 宾语从句（the weekend was itself an invention）
  • resisted fiercely by...：过去分词短语作后置定语，修饰 an invention（曾被激烈抵制）
  • before becoming...：介词短语，"（先被抵制）而后才成为常规"——注意 before doing 的时间层次
译：倡导者指出，双休日本身也是一项发明：一个世纪前曾遭雇主激烈抵制，后来才成为无人质疑的常规。
For sectors where output is tied to hours of physical presence, a shorter week means either hiring more staff or serving fewer people — both of which carry costs that someone must eventually absorb.
  • For sectors [where...]：介词短语+where 定语从句限定范围（"对于产出和在场时长挂钩的行业"）
  • 主干：a shorter week means either A or B（means 后接两个动名词并列）
  • — both of which...：破折号+非限定性定语从句，指代前面 A、B 两件事；which 从句里又套一个 that 定语从句修饰 costs
译：对产出与在场时长挂钩的行业，一周变短意味着要么多雇人、要么少服务——两者都有代价，而代价最终总得有人承担。
Whether other industries can follow will depend less on ideology than on a patient, sector-by-sector reckoning with what work actually consists of — a question the five-day week allowed us, for a century, to avoid asking.
  • Whether... ：主语从句作全句主语
  • depend less on A than on B："与其说取决于 A，不如说取决于 B"——写作高分句型
  • reckoning with + what 宾语从句："对……的清算/审视"
  • — a question (that) ... ：同位语，that 被省略；插入语 for a century 隔开了 allowed us to avoid asking，还原：allowed us to avoid asking (this question) for a century
译：其他行业能否跟进，与其说取决于理念，不如说取决于逐行业耐心审视"工作究竟由什么构成"——这个问题我们借着五天工作制回避了一个世纪。

05可搬进作文/翻译的素材

A depend less on X than on Y
"与其说取决于X，不如说取决于Y" —— 大作文议论万能句型：Success depends less on talent than on sustained effort.
on the condition that ...
"条件是……" —— 比 if 更书面：I will lend it to you on the condition that you return it by Friday.
X is not a universal remedy, but ...
"X不是万能药，但……" —— 让步转折表观点，态度立显客观：Technology is not a universal remedy, but it has punctured the assumption that ...
puncture the assumption that ...
"戳破……的假设" —— 替换低级的 "prove ... wrong"，阅卷眼前一亮。
What is marketed as X may in practice amount to Y
"被包装成X的东西实际上可能等于Y" —— 批判性论证利器：What is marketed as convenience may in practice amount to dependence.
neither utopian nor dismissive
"既不空想也不否定" —— 结尾段表明理性立场；同款结构 neither ... nor ... 任意换词。
a relic of the industrial age / an unquestioned norm
名词短语积累："工业时代的遗物"/"无人质疑的常规" —— 科技、传统类话题都用得上。
