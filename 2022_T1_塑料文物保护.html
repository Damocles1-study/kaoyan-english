Plastics in Peril: 塑料文物保护

2022 年英语（一）真题 · Text 1 · 约 390 词 · 建议做题 18 min · 话题：科技×文化保护（科普说明文）
正确答案：21A 22C 23D 24D 25B题型：细节×3 + 观点细节 + 观点推断难度：中等偏易（2022 四篇中最友好）

01原文精读

🟡 黄色高亮 = 重难点词/短语，点击右侧出卡片 ｜ 蓝色虚线 = 长难句，点击跳到精析 ｜ 每段「译」= 段落译文 ｜ 选中任意其他单词浮出「查有道」

①People often complain that plastics are too durable. Water bottles, shopping bags, and other trash litter the planet, from Mount Everest to the Mariana Trench, because plastics are everywhere and don't break down easily. But some plastic materials change over time. They crack and frizzle. They "weep" out additives. They melt into sludge. All of which creates huge headaches for institutions, such as museums, trying to preserve culturally important objects. The variety of plastic objects at risk is dizzying: early radios, avant-garde sculptures, celluloid animation stills from Disney films, the first artificial heart.

人们常抱怨塑料太耐用了。水瓶、购物袋等垃圾从珠峰到马里亚纳海沟弄脏了整个星球——塑料无处不在，还不易分解。但有些塑料材料会随时间变化：它们开裂、蜷缩，"渗出"添加剂，熔成泥浆。这一切给博物馆等试图保存文化珍品的机构带来巨大的头疼事。处于风险中的塑料物品五花八门得令人眼花缭乱：早期收音机、先锋派雕塑、迪士尼电影的赛璐珞动画定格片、第一颗人工心脏。

②Certain artifacts are especially vulnerable because some pioneers in plastic art didn't always know how to mix ingredients properly, says Thea van Oosten, a polymer chemist who, until retiring a few years ago, worked for decades at the Cultural Heritage Agency of the Netherlands. "It's like baking a cake: If you don't have exact amounts, it goes wrong," she says. "The object you make is already a time bomb."

聚合物化学家 Thea van Oosten 说，某些人工制品格外脆弱，因为塑料艺术的一些先驱并不总是知道如何正确调配原料。她几年前才退休，此前在荷兰文化遗产局工作了数十年。"这就像烤蛋糕：配料不精确就会失败，"她说，"你做出来的东西本身就已经是一颗定时炸弹。"

③And sometimes, it's not the artist's fault. In the 1960s, the Italian artist Piero Gilardi began to create hundreds of bright, colorful foam pieces. Those pieces included small beds of roses and other items as well as a few dozen "nature carpets"—large rectangles decorated with foam pumpkins, cabbages, and watermelons. He wanted viewers to walk around on the carpets—which meant they had to be durable.

而有时这并不是艺术家的错。1960 年代，意大利艺术家 Piero Gilardi 开始创作数百件明亮多彩的泡沫作品，包括小玫瑰花坛等物件，还有几十张"自然地毯"——装饰着泡沫南瓜、卷心菜和西瓜的大长方形。他想让观众在地毯上来回走动——这意味着它们必须耐用。

④Unfortunately, the polyurethane foam he used is inherently unstable. It's especially vulnerable to light damage, and by the mid-1990s, Gilardi's pumpkins, roses, and other figures were splitting and crumbling. Museums locked some of them away in the dark.

不幸的是，他所用的聚氨酯泡沫先天就不稳定，尤其怕光。到 1990 年代中期，Gilardi 的南瓜、玫瑰等造型已经在开裂、碎裂。博物馆只好把其中一些锁进暗处。

⑤So van Oosten and her colleagues worked to preserve Gilardi's sculptures. They infused some with stabilizing and consolidating chemicals. Van Oosten calls those chemicals "sunscreens" because their goal was to prevent further light damage and rebuild worn polymer fibers. She is proud that several sculptures have even gone on display again, albeit sometimes beneath protective cases.

于是 van Oosten 和同事们着手保护 Gilardi 的雕塑。他们给一些作品注入了起稳定和加固作用的化学物质。van Oosten 称这些化学物质为"防晒霜"，因为其目的是防止进一步的光损伤、重建磨损的聚合物纤维。让她自豪的是，有几件雕塑甚至重新展出了——尽管有时得罩在保护罩里。

⑥Despite success stories like van Oosten's, preservation of plastics will likely get harder. Old objects continue to deteriorate. Worse, biodegradable plastics, designed to disintegrate, are increasingly common.

尽管有 van Oosten 这样的成功案例，塑料的保存工作大概率会越来越难：旧物件在继续劣化；更糟的是，天生被设计成会分解的可降解塑料正越来越普遍。

⑦And more is at stake here than individual objects. Joana Lia Ferreira, an assistant professor of conservation and restoration at the NOVA School of Science and Technology, notes that archaeologists first defined the great material ages of human history—Stone Age, Iron Age, and so on—after examining artifacts in museums. We now live in an age of plastic, she says, "and what we decide to collect today, what we decide to preserve...will have a strong impact on how in the future we'll be seen."

而这里攸关的远不止一件件物品。NOVA 理工学院文物保护与修复专业助理教授 Joana Lia Ferreira 指出，考古学家正是在检视博物馆藏品之后，才划定了人类历史的各大"材料时代"——石器时代、铁器时代等等。她说，我们如今生活在塑料时代，"我们今天决定收藏什么、保存什么……将深刻影响未来的人如何看待我们。"

02题目与解析（点选项即判 · 再看解析）

细节题21. According to Paragraph 1, museums are faced with difficulties in ______.

[A] maintaining their plastic items
[B] obtaining durable plastic artifacts
[C] handling outdated plastic exhibits
[D] classifying their plastic collections
答案 A　定位①段中部：All of which creates huge headaches for institutions, such as museums, trying to preserve culturally important objects。塑料会开裂、渗出、熔化 → 给"试图保存"的博物馆制造大麻烦。同义替换：preserve → maintaining，huge headaches → difficulties。
干扰项：B 偷换首句——"too durable"是大众对塑料垃圾的抱怨，不是博物馆想"获得"耐用制品；C "outdated（过时的）"无中生有，文中是"老化受损"不是"过时"；D "分类"无中生有（variety 是说种类多，不是分类难）。

观点细节22. Van Oosten believes that certain plastic objects are ______.

[A] immune to decay
[B] improperly shaped
[C] inherently flawed
[D] complex in structure
答案 C　定位②段：pioneers ... didn't always know how to mix ingredients properly + The object you make is already a time bomb。配方一开始就不对 → 从出生起就带着缺陷 = inherently flawed（先天有缺陷）。"time bomb"这个比喻就是"先天注定要出事"。
干扰项：A 反向（immune to decay = 不会坏，恰恰相反）；B 偷换——问题在 ingredients（配方成分）不在 shape（造型）；D "结构复杂"无中生有。
技巧：人物观点题先圈人名 van Oosten，她的话集中在②⑤段；比喻句（cake / time bomb）几乎必出题，要能"翻译"比喻背后的直义。

细节因果23. Museums stopped exhibiting some of Gilardi's artworks to ______.

[A] keep them from hurting visitors
[B] duplicate them for future display
[C] have their ingredients analyzed
[D] prevent them from further damage
答案 D　定位④段：It's especially vulnerable to light damage ... Museums locked some of them away in the dark。因为怕光损伤 → 锁进暗处 = 防止进一步损坏。⑤段 van Oosten 的"sunscreens"目标 to prevent further light damage 是同一逻辑的显性表达。
干扰项：A "伤到观众"无中生有（作品自己碎，不伤人）；B "复制"无中生有；C 张冠李戴——分析成分是化学家的工作，不是博物馆锁起来的目的。
技巧：问目的（to do）的题，找原文的因果链：怕光 →（所以）锁进黑暗。答案 = 原因的正向改写。

作者态度24. The author thinks that preservation of plastics is ______.

[A] costly
[B] unworthy
[C] unpopular
[D] challenging
答案 D　定位⑥段（作者自己发声的段落）：preservation of plastics will likely get harder ... Worse, biodegradable plastics ... are increasingly common。"会更难 + 更糟" = challenging（充满挑战）。①段的 huge headaches、②段的 time bomb 都在铺垫同一基调。
干扰项：A 全文没谈钱；B 反向——⑦段专门论证保存塑料意义重大，绝非"不值得"；C "不受欢迎"无中生有。
技巧：区分"作者认为"和"人物认为"：本题问 the author，⑥段没有引号、没有人名，正是作者直接评论的段落。

观点推断25. In Ferreira's opinion, preservation of plastic artifacts ______.

[A] will inspire future scientific research
[B] has profound historical significance
[C] will help us separate the material ages
[D] has an impact on today's cultural life
答案 B　定位⑦段：考古学家靠博物馆藏品划定了石器、铁器等"材料时代"；如今是塑料时代，what we decide to preserve...will have a strong impact on how in the future we'll be seen —— 今天保存的塑料 = 未来人认识我们这个时代的史料 → 历史意义深远（profound historical significance）。
干扰项：C 偷换时态兼张冠李戴——"划定时代"是考古学家过去已完成的事，不是塑料保存将来的作用；D 偷换时间——影响的是 in the future 如何看我们，不是"今天的文化生活"；A "激发科研"无中生有。
技巧：末段人物观点题 ≈ 全文升华题，答案往往是引语的"抽象一层"改写：具体（影响未来怎么看我们）→ 抽象（历史意义）。

03长难句精析（正文蓝色虚线句）

All of which creates huge headaches for institutions, such as museums, trying to preserve culturally important objects.
  • 特殊现象：which 指代前面三个短句的全部内容（开裂/渗出/熔化），按语法本应接在逗号后，这里是外刊常见的修辞性断句（= And all of this creates...）
  • such as museums：插入举例，隔开了 institutions 和它的修饰语
  • trying to preserve...：现在分词短语作后置定语，修饰 institutions（"那些试图保存文化珍品的机构"）——21 题答案藏在这里
译：这一切都给博物馆等试图保存文化珍品的机构带来巨大的麻烦。
Certain artifacts are especially vulnerable because some pioneers in plastic art didn't always know how to mix ingredients properly, says Thea van Oosten, a polymer chemist who, until retiring a few years ago, worked for decades at the Cultural Heritage Agency of the Netherlands.
  • 新闻体引语倒装：整个前半句是说话内容，says Thea van Oosten 后置（= Thea van Oosten says that certain artifacts are...）
  • didn't always know：部分否定，"并不总是知道"，≠"总是不知道"
  • a polymer chemist：同位语，交代身份
  • who, until retiring a few years ago, worked...：定语从句中插入时间状语，隔开了 who 和 worked——还原：who worked for decades ... until retiring（工作数十年，直到几年前退休）
译：聚合物化学家 Thea van Oosten 说，某些制品格外脆弱，因为塑料艺术的一些先驱并不总是知道如何正确调配原料；她几年前退休，此前在荷兰文化遗产局工作了数十年。
Joana Lia Ferreira, an assistant professor of conservation and restoration at the NOVA School of Science and Technology, notes that archaeologists first defined the great material ages of human history—Stone Age, Iron Age, and so on—after examining artifacts in museums.
  • 主干：Ferreira notes that + 宾语从句；主语和谓语之间隔着长同位语（身份介绍）
  • 破折号对：插入列举（石器时代、铁器时代等），阅读时可先跳过
  • after examining...：时间状语——先看博物馆藏品，才定义时代 → 藏品是划定历史的依据（25 题推理的根基）
  • 配套引语：what we decide to collect today, what we decide to preserve... 两个 what 主语从句排比作主语；how in the future we'll be seen 中 in the future 插在 how 与主谓之间
译：Ferreira 指出，考古学家是在检视博物馆藏品之后，才划定了人类历史的各大材料时代——石器时代、铁器时代等等。

05可搬进作文/翻译的素材

And more is at stake here than X
"攸关的远不止X" —— 议论文升华段神句：More is at stake here than convenience; what hangs in the balance is our attention itself.
albeit + 短语
"尽管"的高级版（只接短语不接句子）：Progress has been made, albeit slowly. 阅卷人眼前一亮的小词。
inherently + adj.
"先天就……"：Social media is not inherently harmful; it depends on how we use it. 替换 naturally/in itself。
The variety of X is dizzying: A, B, C...
总起+列举的漂亮写法："种类多得令人眼花缭乱"，冒号后接三四个例子，图表作文描述多样性可用。
X is already a time bomb
比喻：定时炸弹——描述隐患类话题（食品安全、拖延、环境）通用。
What we decide to X today will have a strong impact on how in the future we'll be seen
排比 what 从句作主语 + 深远影响句式——文化传承/环保话题结尾段直接套用。
deteriorate / preservation / cultural heritage
话题词打包记：文物保护、环境恶化类文章的固定班底。
